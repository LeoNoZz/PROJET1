Legendary riders

Christian Léon

Christian Léon, born May 11, 1948 in Osny, is a French motorcycle pilot. He is best known for his performances in endurance races, notably at the 24 Hours of Le Mans. He won the race twice, in 1978 and 1979, alongside his accomplice Jean-Claude Chemarin. He also won the Bol d'Or three times, in 1978, 1979 and 1980. Unfortunately, Christian Léon died on November 8, 1980 following an accident during private testing in Japan.

Jean-Claude Chemarin

Jean-Claude Chemarin, born May 6, 1952 in Meudon, is a French motorcycle racing driver. He is best known for his performances in endurance races, notably at the 24 Hours of Le Mans. He has won the Bol d'Or in the premier class four times, and has also won races such as the 24 Hours of Liège. He competed in the 24 Hours of Le Mans several times, but never won the race.

Alex Vieira

Alex Vieira is a French motorcycle racer who made his mark in the history of the 24 Hours of Le Mans Moto. He is the record holder for victories in this race with five successes, a record he shares with Grégory Leblanc. He also won the FIM Endurance Cup twice (1989 and 1990) and was crowned World Endurance Champion in 1991.
